# High-Converting Service Websites

When building websites for local or remote service-based businesses (e.g., home services, agencies, consulting), the primary goal is to convert visitors into booked appointments or leads.

1. **The "Above the Fold" Promise**: 
   The hero section must instantly answer three questions: What do you do? Where do you do it? How do I get it? Include a clear value proposition and a low-friction Call to Action (CTA) like "Get a Free Quote" or "Book Now".
2. **Eliminate Friction**: 
   Do not force users to create accounts just to contact you. Use simple lead forms or direct "Click to Call" buttons. Ensure phone numbers auto-format as `tel:` links.
3. **Social Proof as Architecture**: 
   Reviews should not be hidden on a separate page. Inject undeniable proof (Google Reviews, Before/After sliders, Case Studies) immediately below the hero section or woven alongside the service descriptions.
4. **Transparent Pricing or Process**: 
   Fear of the unknown kills conversions. Clearly outline the process ("1. Get Quote, 2. We Work, 3. You Relax") and mention pricing transparency ("Honest pricing, no bait-and-switch").
5. **Sticky CTAs**: 
   A mobile screen is small; always keep a floating "Call Now" or "Get Quote" button anchored to the bottom or top of the viewport on mobile devices so the user never has to scroll to convert.

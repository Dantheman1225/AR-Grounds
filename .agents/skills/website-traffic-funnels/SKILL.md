# Website Traffic Funnels

A website should operate like a digital salesperson. Unstructured websites lose traffic; funnels direct it.

1. **The Single Objective**: 
   Every page must have a primary objective. Do not present the user with 10 equal options. Highlight the primary action (e.g., "Add to Cart", "Book Consultation") and demote secondary actions via ghost buttons or text links.
2. **Value Ladders**: 
   Not all traffic is ready to buy immediately. Provide a low-friction entry point (e.g., a free PDF, a quiz, a free estimate form) to capture an email address or phone number for retargeting before asking for a high-ticket sale.
3. **Micro-Commitments**: 
   Instead of presenting a daunting 15-field form, use a multi-step form. Ask for the Zip Code first. Once they click "Next," they are psychologically committed to finishing the process.
4. **Urgency and Scarcity (Authentic)**: 
   Leverage real scarcity to push off-the-fence buyers. Example: "Same-week scheduling available—only 2 slots left in [City]" or highlight fast turnaround times.
5. **Post-Conversion Ascension**: 
   Never waste a "Thank You" page. Once a lead is captured or a purchase is made, immediately present them with an upsell, a referral program, or a request to follow the brand's social channels.
